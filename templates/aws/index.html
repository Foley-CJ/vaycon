NoSQL Databases Analysis: Twitter Utilization


NoSQL databases have been gaining popularity ever since they first appeared in 1998. Since then, multiple offerings have entered the space, each with a slightly different value proposition. Given the uniqueness of each system, it can be challenging to determine which product to deploy. One dimension of that comparison is the level of support and community.

In this analysis, we investigate the breadth of the community by evaluating how three of the most popular NoSQL databases utilize Twitter. More specifically, we evaluate MongoDB, DynamoDB, and Cassandra by pulling data with the Twitter API and investigating it with python's Plotly package.


High Level Product Summary


To start this analysis, we begin by reviewing a few key facts about the three products. These key facts include the database's release date, when it joined Twitter, the number of followers, total tweets sent, and tweets sent within 2021.

Database Initial Release Twitter Date Followers Total Tweets 2021 Tweets
Cassandra 2008 2009 40.7K 479 117
DynamoDB 2012 2008 16.5K 6.3K 1180
MongoDB 2009 2008 381.5K 20.8K 1601

These figures reveal that neither release date nor tweet quantity alone can predict the number of followers, the key performance indicator that we're interested in exploring. Additionally, this data suggests that while DynamoDB and Cassandra have been less engaged than MongoDB, they are taking a more active role in 2021.

    Results: 2021 Analysis


    There are multiple takeaways and additional opportunities for further analysis surrounding this investigation. The three major takeaways from this analysis include:

    • MongoDB is effective at drawing engagement in the form of likes and growing its follower base by utilizing question-based tweets.
    • Utilizing live events such as MongoDB.live or MondoDB.local in conjunction with Twitter works well to build a platform and extend the reach of that platform. AWS could leverage its AWS re:Invent conference to the same extent.
    • Since September of this year, DynamoDBs tweets have significantly dropped off. This shift has allowed other communities to grow in their place.

    Some additional opportunities and outstanding questions that should still be addressed as as follows:

    • Do feature/product enhancement announcements draw an impactful engagement from Twitter followers?
    • Does the sentiment or style of communications have a significant impact on the effectiveness of Twitter?
    • Are there alternative social media applications that could provide a more valuable return on the amount of time invested?
    • Does success in the Twitter community relate to success in the real community for the database?

    Ultimately this analysis revealed that MongoDB is using a variety of tactics to engage with its community, that social network dynamics is not a perfect science, and that all three products have opportunities to improve their community outreach.
